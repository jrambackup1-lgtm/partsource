## 1. Verified Customer Pain Points (Voice of the Engineer)

The following verbatim posts validate the high-friction areas in mechanical procurement.

### Pain Point 1: McMaster Price Markup & Shipping Opaque Costs
> *"The convenience of McMasterCarr is the convenience, but it's heavily marked up and the shipping can be pricey."*
> — **r/engineering** ("Cheaper alternatives to McMaster Carr?"), Apr 2016.
- **Context**: Shipping costs are only calculated *after* placing the order on McMaster. For small teams or prototype runs, the markup and shipping fee are highly punitive.

### Pain Point 2: Shipping Small Orders Sting
> *"I ordered two pairs of unthreaded spacers... Sucks though, like $20 in shipping for 4 small parts."*
> — **r/AskEngineers** ("WTF is up with McMaster-Carr?"), Sep 2020.
- **Context**: A $10 order costing $20 in ground shipping. Engineers are willing to find alternatives if a tool lists them instantly.

### Pain Point 3: Search Filters Prevent Cost Sorting
> *"Often there are many items meeting the specs I want but I just want to see which ones are the cheapest... You can't sort by price."*
> — **Hacker News** ("Amazon Isn't the Only Shop Online"), Apr 2020.
- **Context**: McMaster-Carr's search design explicitly hides prices until you locate the exact spec. Users cannot sort by price to optimize budgets.

### Pain Point 4: Explicit Demand for Sourcing Tool
> *"Is there a tool out there that lets you quickly price compare McMaster to other suppliers for a given part number? e.g. you put in the McMaster part number and it tells you a price for an equivalent product at Grainger or BoltDepot?"*
> — **r/manufacturing** ("Price Compare to McMaster Carr"), Feb 2021.
- **Context**: This is the exact value proposition of partsource.io. The thread confirmed no such tool existed.

### Pain Point 5: Corporate Procurement Constraints
> *"Literally 99% of what I purchase is from McMaster... My frustration with my employer is that they are a multi billion $ company. They at the top decided this [phasing out McMaster]. And tell us to figure it out."*
> — **Practical Machinist** ("Is there a good alternative to McMaster Carr?"), Dec 2023.
- **Context**: Enterprise finance departments block McMaster purchases due to billing terms. Engineers are forced to find equivalents on other approved platforms.

## 3. Critical Factual Corrections

Factual errors corrected and verified via primary sources (10-K filings, corporate filings, acquisition history).

1. **McMaster-Carr Ownership**: McMaster is **NOT owned by Hillman Solutions**. They are separate, competing companies. McMaster is private, family-owned, and does not file public accounts (revenue estimated at $3B–$5B). Hillman is public (HLMN) and files audited accounts ($1.47B sales in FY2025).
2. **Octopart Acquisition History**: Octopart was acquired by **Altium Limited in August 2015** (not Nexar in 2021). Nexar is simply a brand/platform owned by Altium. Altium was subsequently acquired by Renesas Electronics in August 2024.
3. **OSH Cut Independence**: OSH Cut remains a fully independent company owned by its founders (the Chamberlain brothers). It was **NOT acquired by Protolabs**. (Protolabs acquired 3D Hubs in 2021 for $280M).
