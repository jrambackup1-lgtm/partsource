### Industrial E-Commerce Penetration
- **Digitization Gradient**: Commodity MRO (fasteners, safety gear) has high online purchasing rates (**30–50%**). Engineered components (bearings, pneumatic cylinders) have lower rates (**10–20%**).
- **Buying Behavior**: **65% of industrial buyers** buy online for at least some orders. Digital transactions account for **~16% of total B2B industrial spend** by volume.
- **Growth Trend**: Grainger's digital-only e-commerce segment (Zoro + MonotaRO) grew **7.5% YoY**, outperforming traditional high-touch field sales (3.4% growth).

### Implication for partsource.io
On-demand manufacturing platforms command high multiples and take-rates because they eliminate friction for small-order prototype engineers. `partsource.io` targets the same audience (low-volume hardware developers) but with a much lower overhead broker model, routing orders instead of executing manufacturing directly.

## 3. Venture Capital Landscape (2024-2026)

Active investors and recent venture deals in B2B supply chain, industrial MRO, and agentic procurement SaaS.

### Active VCs & Stated Portfolios
- **Andreessen Horowitz (a16z)**: Thesis points to enterprise procurement as the last manual back-office bastion. Backed **Lio** ($30M Series A, Mar 2026).
- **Bessemer Venture Partners**: Maintains "Supply Chain Software Roadmap." Backed **MaintainX** ($150M Series D, Jul 2025) and **GoodShip** ($8M Series A, May 2024).
- **Chemistry**: Backed **Didero** ($30M Series A, Feb 2026) for agentic manufacturing procurement.
- **Lux Capital**: Focused on "transforming atoms as fluidly as bits." Backed **Hadrian** ($260M Series C, Jul 2025) and **SkySelect** ($9M, Mar 2026).
- **Ironspring Ventures**: A $100M sector-focused fund dedicated entirely to digital industrial innovation.

### The procurement gap
While VCs are funding multi-million dollar AI agent tools (Didero, Lio, Pelico), **100% of these tools target enterprise procurement (ERPs, Fortune 500 supply chains)**. None are servicing the fast-moving hardware prototype and startup market. `partsource.io`'s free-decoder-to-BOM-brokerage model targets this underserved wedge.
